# Hermetic Principles & Sciences

## The Seven Hermetic Principles

### 1. The Principle of Mentalism
"The All is Mind; the Universe is Mental"

- The universe is mental in nature
- Reality originates from consciousness/mind
- Individual minds are part of the Universal Mind
- Thought creates reality
- **Application**: Focused intention and visualization for personal transformation

### 2. The Principle of Correspondence
"As above, so below; as below, so above"

- Patterns repeat at all levels of existence
- Macrocosm and microcosm are reflections
- Understanding one level helps understand others
- What happens in the body reflects the universe; what happens in the universe reflects in the body
- **Application**: Self-study as a mirror to understand universal principles

### 3. The Principle of Vibration
"Nothing rests; everything moves; everything vibrates"

- All is in motion at different frequencies
- Everything vibrates at its own rate
- Raising or lowering vibration changes circumstances
- Thought, emotion, and physical matter all vibrate
- **Application**: Meditation and energy work to elevate personal vibration

### 4. The Principle of Polarity
"Everything is dual; everything has poles; everything has its pair of opposites"

- All things have two poles of the same thing
- Opposites are identical in nature but different in degree
- Contraries are reconciliable
- **Application**: Understanding complementary forces (yin-yang, masculine-feminine, etc.)

### 5. The Principle of Rhythm
"Everything flows, out and in; everything has its tides"

- Everything moves in cycles
- Between the two poles there is always a rhythm of motion
- Understanding rhythm allows for riding the tide of change
- Seasons, biological cycles, historical cycles all follow rhythmic patterns
- **Application**: Working with natural cycles and seasons for optimal outcomes

### 6. The Principle of Cause and Effect
"Every cause has its effect; every effect has its cause"

- Nothing happens by chance
- Everything follows law
- There are many planes of causation
- Understanding cause reveals effect; understanding effect reveals cause
- **Application**: Taking responsibility for creating reality through conscious choice

### 7. The Principle of Gender
"Gender is in everything; everything has its Masculine and Feminine principles"

- All things contain both masculine (active, generative) and feminine (receptive, generative) principles
- These are not limited to biological sex
- Balance of these principles is necessary for creation and wholeness
- **Application**: Integrating masculine and feminine energies within and without

## The Four Elements

### Fire
- **Qualities**: Warmth, expansion, transformation, willpower
- **Direction**: South
- **Season**: Summer
- **Time of Day**: Noon
- **Tarot Suit**: Wands
- **Associated Herbs**: Ginger, cayenne, turmeric, cinnamon
- **Element Work**: Intention setting, manifestation, courage building

### Air
- **Qualities**: Movement, communication, intellect, clarity
- **Direction**: East
- **Season**: Spring
- **Time of Day**: Dawn
- **Tarot Suit**: Swords
- **Associated Herbs**: Lavender, mint, rosemary, eucalyptus
- **Element Work**: Clear thinking, communication, perspective shift

### Water
- **Qualities**: Emotional flow, intuition, healing, purification
- **Direction**: West
- **Season**: Autumn
- **Time of Day**: Dusk
- **Tarot Suit**: Cups
- **Associated Herbs**: Chamomile, rose, hibiscus, jasmine
- **Element Work**: Emotional healing, intuition development, cleansing

### Earth
- **Qualities**: Stability, grounding, manifestation, material reality
- **Direction**: North
- **Season**: Winter
- **Time of Day**: Midnight
- **Tarot Suit**: Pentacles
- **Associated Herbs**: Vetiver, patchouli, oak moss, cedarwood
- **Element Work**: Grounding, manifestation, stability building

## The Chakra System

### Root Chakra (Muladhara)
- **Color**: Red
- **Element**: Earth
- **Associated Herbs**: Rose, ginger, vetiver
- **Function**: Grounding, safety, survival

### Sacral Chakra (Svadhisthana)
- **Color**: Orange
- **Element**: Water
- **Associated Herbs**: Jasmine, cinnamon, orange
- **Function**: Creativity, sexuality, pleasure

### Solar Plexus Chakra (Manipura)
- **Color**: Yellow
- **Element**: Fire
- **Associated Herbs**: Turmeric, lemon, ginger
- **Function**: Personal power, will, transformation

### Heart Chakra (Anahata)
- **Color**: Green
- **Element**: Air/Water
- **Associated Herbs**: Rose, jasmine, lavender
- **Function**: Love, compassion, connection

### Throat Chakra (Vishuddha)
- **Color**: Blue
- **Element**: Air
- **Associated Herbs**: Lavender, mint, sage
- **Function**: Communication, truth, expression

### Third Eye Chakra (Ajna)
- **Color**: Indigo
- **Element**: Light
- **Associated Herbs**: Mugwort, rosemary, gotu kola
- **Function**: Intuition, insight, inner wisdom

### Crown Chakra (Sahasrara)
- **Color**: Violet/White
- **Element**: Thought
- **Associated Herbs**: Lavender, frankincense
- **Function**: Spiritual connection, unity consciousness

## Sacred Geometry

### The Flower of Life
- Ancient sacred symbol found in many cultures
- Represents the fundamental forms of space and time
- Used for meditation and spiritual awakening

### The Vesica Piscis
- Formed by two overlapping circles
- Sacred feminine symbol
- Represents the union of opposites

### The Golden Ratio (Phi)
- Mathematical proportion appearing throughout nature
- Associated with beauty and harmony
- Used in design and architecture of sacred spaces

### The Platonic Solids
- Five geometric forms representing the elements
- Used in meditation and spiritual work
- **Tetrahedron**: Fire
- **Cube**: Earth
- **Octahedron**: Air
- **Icosahedron**: Water
- **Dodecahedron**: The Universe/Void
